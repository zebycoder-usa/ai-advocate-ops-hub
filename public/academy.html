Getting Started & Eligibility

What Upwork actually is, who is allowed to use it, and the Connects you spend to bid. Start here before anything else.

180+ countries18+ only$0.15 per ConnectPlus $19.99/mo

How Upwork works

A global marketplace that connects freelancers with clients who post paid work.

The freelancer flow

  • Build a strong profile, find jobs, send a tailored proposal, deliver, and get paid securely.
  • Everything runs on-platform: messaging, contracts, and payments all live inside Upwork.
  • Staying on-platform is what keeps payment protection and dispute help available to you.

How matching works

  • Upwork surfaces jobs to you and you to clients via a Best Match system built on profile and job-post data.
  • The exact ranking is not published, by design, so it cannot be gamed.
  • Practical takeaway: an accurate, keyword-rich profile and post-specific proposals are what move you up.
Why it matters
On-platform is not a formality. Off-platform contact or payment voids protection and can close the account. Keep every step inside Upwork.

Who can join

Upwork serves 180+ countries, but not everywhere, and a few hard rules apply to everyone.

Baseline requirements

  • Be 18+ (or the age of majority where you live), whichever is older.
  • You are responsible for your own work-authorization rules.
  • Upwork cannot advise whether you personally may use it beyond the listed restrictions.

Restricted by US law

  • Russia and Belarus — operations suspended since May 1, 2022.
  • Iran, North Korea, Cuba (and Cuban nationals), Crimea, LPR, DPR — cannot register or use the platform.
  • Anyone on the US SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) list is excluded; the list changes over time.

If you were suspended before

  • A previously suspended user is not allowed back.
  • New accounts opened by a suspended user get closed.
  • This is why protecting account standing matters so much (see Topic H).

Connects — the bidding currency

Connects are what you spend to submit and boost proposals.

How they work

  • Each Connect costs $0.15, bought in bundles; a verified billing method is required.
  • A standard proposal costs about 6 Connects; larger/expert jobs can cost more.
  • The exact cost is shown on each post and can change while the job is live — always check.

Free allowances & refunds

  • Free monthly Connects: Basic 10 · Freelancer Plus 100.
  • Connects are refunded if a client withdraws the post.
  • They are not refunded for jobs you simply do not win — treat them as marketing spend.

Freelancer Plus

An optional membership that adds Connects and visibility tools.

What $19.99/month buys

  • About 100 Connects each cycle, full access to Uma (Upwork AI), and proposal/job insights.
  • See competitor bid ranges and client activity before you bid.
  • 0% freelancer fee on Direct Contracts while the plan is active.
  • Cancel anytime to drop back to Basic.

★ Key takeaways

  • Everything — chat, contracts, payment — stays on Upwork; off-platform breaks protection.
  • You must be 18+ and outside the restricted regions; suspended users cannot return.
  • Connects cost $0.15; a standard bid is ~6; only client withdrawals refund them.
  • Free Connects: 10/mo Basic, 100/mo Plus.
  • Freelancer Plus ($19.99) adds Connects, insights, and 0% Direct-Contract fees.

Profile & Verification

A complete, verified profile is your storefront — it drives visibility, trust, and hire rate. Here is how to build and verify it.

100% profile goal1 account, many profilesBlue ID badgeAvailable now badge

Build a 100% profile

Completeness is a ranking and trust signal — finish every part.

What to fill in

  • Work history, education & certifications, languages, availability, and a real portfolio.
  • A specialized profile focused on one niche outperforms a generic one.
  • Add genuine samples — screenshots, links, case studies with real numbers.

Writing the bio

  • Lead with action verbs and industry keywords clients actually search.
  • State one clear value proposition, not a list of everything you can do.
  • For Saqib: lead with true AI/LLM/RAG/FastAPI metrics and the MS in Data Science — never a credential not on the live profile.
Honesty gate
Never state a degree, title, employer, or metric that is not on the live profile. A client who verifies a false claim is a lost client and an account risk.

One account, many profiles

You do not need multiple logins — one account holds several profiles.

The structure

  • One account per login/email holds one freelancer + one agency + multiple client profiles.
  • Each profile keeps its own contracts, finances, and history.
  • Switch between them from the dropdown under your profile photo.

The hard rule

  • Creating multiple separate accounts violates the Terms of Service.
  • It can trigger permanent closure.
  • Need a second identity (e.g. an agency)? Add a profile inside the one account instead.

Identity verification

Verifying identity earns a trust badge and unlocks certain work.

How you verify

  • The blue identity-verification badge signals you are a real, checked person.
  • Methods: government ID, sometimes ID plus proof of address, a live camera/video check, and phone number.
  • Some US-only jobs require location verification; KYC info may be requested.

Camera tips

  • Allow browser/app camera access when prompted — it is required to verify.
  • If the camera fails: check permissions, try another browser, or use the mobile app.
  • The badge is paid in Connects, not cash.

The Availability Badge

A paid signal that tells clients you are ready for work now.

What it does

  • Adds a blue 'Available now' badge to your profile, visible in search.
  • Clients can filter by an 'Available now' filter when inviting talent.
  • Goal: earn more invites with less effort.

What it costs

  • Bought with Connects; the weekly price varies with demand.
  • You set a maximum you are willing to spend.
  • Clients do not see that you paid for it.

Account controls

A few settings worth knowing.

Preferences

  • Toggle dark/light mode and manage your AI preferences in settings.
  • Control how your name is displayed to clients.
  • Keep contact details out of your public profile until a contract exists.

Using a VPN

  • A VPN is allowed only if used properly.
  • Never use one to fake your location or bypass restrictions.
  • Location misrepresentation is an account-standing risk.

★ Key takeaways

  • A 100% profile in one clear niche wins more visibility and trust.
  • Use one account with multiple profiles — never a second account.
  • The blue identity badge (ID + camera/phone) is a strong trust signal.
  • The Availability Badge buys invites with Connects; price floats with demand.
  • Every claim must match the live profile — no exceptions.

Finding Work & Proposals

How to find the right jobs and win them — search, proposals, boosting (and its placebo auctions), Direct Contracts, Project Catalog, and the Job Success Score.

Boolean searchBoost auction0% Direct Contracts (Plus)JSS 90%+ strong

Job search

Find work that matches your skills, not just your keywords.

How to search

  • Matches on client category, skills, title, and description.
  • Advanced Search supports Boolean: AND / OR / NOT, plus all-words, any-words, and exact-phrase.
  • Ranking is intentionally not fully disclosed, so no trick guarantees the top spot.

Let jobs come to you

  • Set job alerts and the job digest email to surface matches automatically.
  • Save searches for your niche to check quickly each day.
  • Speed matters — early, tailored proposals get seen first.

Sending proposals

A proposal is a bid against one specific post.

The basics

  • The Connect cost is shown on the post; a standard bid is ~6 Connects.
  • Tailor every proposal — mirror the client's own words and name their stack.
  • Freelancer Plus shows competitor bid ranges and client activity to bid smarter.

What wins

  • One true metric that maps to their exact problem.
  • 2–3 exact words lifted from their post.
  • End with one specific question about their project. Never open with 'Dear Hiring Manager'.

Boosted Proposals & placebo auctions

Pay extra Connects to jump to the top of the client's list.

How boosting works

  • Bid extra Connects; boosted proposals show separately at the top for the client.
  • You set a max bid; a bid table shows the ranges others are spending; ties resolve by a set rule.
  • You can update or re-boost; eligibility depends on how strong a match you are.

Placebo auctions

  • Some auctions are placebo — used to calibrate the system, with no real competition.
  • Connects are not taken for a placebo boost.
  • Lesson: boosting is probabilistic, so only boost high-fit jobs where it is worth it.
Boost wisely
Boosting a weak-fit job creates a 'bounce' signal that can lower your ranking. Boost only high-fit jobs in your proven niche.

Direct offers & Direct Contracts

Not all work starts with a proposal.

Direct offers

  • A client can send you a contract offer directly — you negotiate, accept, or decline.
  • Different from an invitation to apply, which still asks for a proposal.
  • You can request changes to an offer before accepting.

Direct Contracts

  • Bring your own off-platform client onto Upwork with payment protection.
  • Reduced fee — 0% for active Freelancer Plus.
  • Moving an existing contract off Upwork costs a conversion fee.

Project Catalog

Sell pre-packaged work instead of bidding.

How it works

  • Publish fixed-price, fixed-timeline project packages clients buy directly.
  • Add images, video, and PDF work samples to sell the package.
  • No proposal or Connects needed — clients purchase and you deliver.

Job Success Score (JSS)

Your track-record number, shown as a percentage.

Reading the bands

  • 90%+ = strong; 80s = solid with room to grow; 79% or below = challenges.
  • Built from client feedback, contract outcomes, and long-term relationships.
  • An agency's JSS is the aggregate of all its jobs — one bad contract affects everyone.

★ Key takeaways

  • Use Boolean search + alerts to find fit fast; bid early and tailored.
  • Win formula: one true metric + their exact words + one sharp question.
  • Boost only high-fit jobs; some auctions are placebo and cost nothing.
  • Direct Contracts bring your own clients on at 0% for Plus; Project Catalog sells packages.
  • Protect JSS 90%+ — for an agency it is a shared, aggregate score.

Payments, Fees & Taxes

What you actually keep, how and when money reaches you, and the tax basics — including the fact that the flat 10% fee is gone.

Fee 0–15%Weekly cycle ~10 daysUS ACH freeUS persons file W-9

Fees (verified July 2026)

The old tiered 20/10/5 structure was retired on May 1, 2025.

Freelancer service fee

  • Variable 0–15% per contract — not a flat 10%.
  • The exact percentage is shown and locked when you submit the proposal.
  • Blended average most agencies pay is ~11–13.5%; scarce/high-demand AI skills can land at the low end.

Other charges

  • Client marketplace fee 3–10% (US ACH gets the lower rate).
  • Contract-initiation fee $0.99–$14.99, charged to the client per new contract.
  • Connects $0.15; standard proposal ~6 Connects ($0.90).
Quoting math
gross = net ÷ (1 − quoted fee). Because the fee is variable, read the exact % on each proposal before quoting a number.

Getting paid & withdrawals

Set up tax info first, then choose how money reaches you.

Setup order

  • Enter tax info first, then add a withdrawal method.
  • Earnings clear after the contract's cycle; a reserve balance can briefly hold funds.
  • Withdrawal methods each have their own timing and fee.

Method timing & fees

  • US ACH: free, 3–5 business days.
  • Pakistan Direct to Local Bank: $0.99, ~4 days, PKR — personal account only (PKR to business accounts is rejected).
  • US wire ~$30; Payoneer / PayPal timing and fees vary; holiday delays can happen.

Weekly billing (hourly)

Hourly work runs on a fixed weekly clock.

The cycle

  • Work week: Mon 00:00 → Sun 23:59 UTC.
  • Client is invoiced Monday and reviews Mon–Fri.
  • Earnings become available the following Wednesday — roughly 10 days end to end.

Taxes

Upwork collects the tax info it is required to.

What to expect

  • Tax info is required before you can withdraw.
  • US persons (including Saqib) complete Form W-9.
  • Elsewhere Upwork handles local taxes: India GST + TDS, Australia/Singapore GST, Switzerland/Colombia/Vietnam VAT, and more.

★ Key takeaways

  • The freelancer fee is variable 0–15%, locked at proposal — the flat 10% is retired.
  • Quote with gross = net ÷ (1 − fee), reading the real % each time.
  • Add tax info first; US ACH is free, Pakistan local bank is $0.99 to a personal account.
  • Hourly money lands about 10 days after the Mon–Sun UTC week.
  • US persons file a W-9; local taxes are handled per country.

Protection & Disputes

How payment protection actually qualifies, what to do when a client goes silent, and how disputes and refunds work.

Hourly: track timeFixed: escrow firstSilent client: 14-day releaseRefund window 180d

Hourly Payment Protection

Protection is not automatic — you have to qualify for it.

How to qualify

  • The client has a verified billing method.
  • You track time with the Desktop App, and the work matches the contract.
  • You stay within the contract's weekly limit.

What is not covered

  • Manual time logged outside these rules is not protected.
  • Work that does not match the contract description is at risk.
  • No verified client billing method = no protection.

Fixed-Price Payment Protection

On fixed work, escrow is everything.

How to qualify

  • Do not start until the milestone is fully funded in project funds (escrow).
  • Only funds actually in escrow are covered.
  • Confirm the client's payment method is verified first.
Rule
No escrow, no start. Unfunded 'we'll pay after' arrangements have no protection and are a classic loss.

When a client goes silent

You are not stuck — each contract type has a path.

Hourly

  • You can end the contract anytime.
  • Properly-logged protected hours still pay on the normal cycle.
  • Keep your work diary clean so those hours hold up.

Fixed-price

  • Complete and submit the milestone (Deliver work → active contracts).
  • Then wait 14 days for automatic release if the client does not respond.
  • Do not end before submitting — that can forfeit the milestone's funds.

Disputes & refunds

When there is genuine disagreement, there is a process.

Filing a dispute

  • Fixed: if a client will not release a funded milestone, file a dispute on an active or ended contract.
  • Upwork offers a non-binding resolution / mediation step.
  • Hourly: a client can dispute your hours — respond with work-diary evidence.

Refunds

  • A client can request a refund within 180 days.
  • The freelancer decides full, partial, or none.
  • Escrow-deposit refunds and hourly disputes are separate flows.

★ Key takeaways

  • Hourly protection needs a verified client + tracked time within the limit.
  • Fixed protection needs funded escrow — never start unfunded.
  • Silent client? Hourly: end anytime. Fixed: submit, then 14-day auto-release.
  • Disputes go through a non-binding resolution; keep work-diary evidence.
  • Clients can request refunds for 180 days; you decide the amount.

Contracts, Time & Feedback

The desktop time tracker and work diary, day-to-day contract management, and the 14-day feedback window that shapes your reputation.

Desktop trackerWork diaryMilestone changesReview 14 days

The Desktop App & time tracker

For hourly work, the desktop app is what makes protection real.

What it does

  • Logs hourly time via the time tracker — screenshots plus activity — building your work diary.
  • Tracked time is what earns full Hourly Protection.
  • Supports manual time too, but manual time has less protection.

Common fixes

  • Download failing or won't log in? Reinstall and re-login.
  • Time or contracts not appearing? Clear cached uploads and re-sync.
  • Seeing only messages, not the tracker? Update the app.

Managing contracts

Tools that keep a contract organized and on-track.

Day to day

  • Choose tracked vs manual time; add activity tags, to-do lists, and use the contract workroom.
  • Propose milestone changes or a new hourly rate from the contract menu.
  • Upwork may pause a contract (billing/verification) until the issue is fixed.

Ending a contract

  • End from the contract menu when the work is complete.
  • A review is part of ending — see below.
  • On fixed work, submit and get paid before ending.

Feedback

Reviews at the end of a contract shape your JSS and reputation.

The review window

  • End-of-contract review has a 14-day window: a public review plus a private note.
  • A review is required to end a contract.
  • Public reviews can be edited within the window.

Good practice

  • Give clients thoughtful feedback too — it builds relationships.
  • If a client left feedback you'd like revisited, you can ask them to update it.
  • Never pressure or trade for reviews — that breaks the rules.

★ Key takeaways

  • The Desktop App + time tracker builds the work diary that protects hourly pay.
  • Manual time is allowed but less protected — track when you can.
  • You can propose milestone and rate changes from the contract.
  • A contract can be paused by Upwork until billing/verification is fixed.
  • The 14-day review window (public + private) shapes your JSS — handle it with care.

Agencies on Upwork

This is AI Advocate's operating model. How agencies work on Upwork — members, the Agency Plus plan, Connects, and finances.

Team under 1 profileAgency Plus $19.99100 agency ConnectsExclusive vs non-exclusive

How agencies work

An agency is a team operating under one profile.

The model

  • Clients hire the agency — the owner, a manager, or an assigned member.
  • Payments go to the agency, which then pays its members by internal agreement.
  • The agency profile lives inside the owner's single Upwork account.

Roles

  • An owner/admin manages the agency, its members, and its finances.
  • Members are added to bid and deliver under the agency.
  • Contracts roll up to the agency's aggregate JSS.

Agency Plus plan

The agency's own membership, separate from a personal one.

What it gives

  • $19.99/month: the agency's own Connects (about 100/cycle) and the ability to bid as the agency.
  • Agency Connects follow cycle-end rules — they do not roll forever.
  • An agency can run alongside a personal Freelancer Plus.

Connects rules

  • Only authorized members can purchase agency Connects.
  • You can submit a proposal with or without an assigned member, per settings.
  • Check who is allowed to submit before a busy bidding day.

Members

Who is on the team, and how tightly they're bound.

Exclusive vs non-exclusive

  • Exclusive members work only under this agency.
  • Non-exclusive members also work independently or with other agencies.
  • Choose based on how the person actually works with you.

Managing the roster

  • Add or remove members as the team changes.
  • Assign a specific member to a proposal, or bid as the agency.
  • Removing a member does not erase past contract history.

Operating an agency

The levers an owner controls.

Settings & finances

  • Manage the agency's rate and visibility, its proposals and offers, and its finances.
  • You can create, close, or leave an agency.
  • Agency contracts function like individual ones but aggregate to the agency.

Trust, Safety & Support

Spotting scams, locking down the account, and knowing where to get help — the safety layer that protects everything else.

Spot red flagsAuthenticator appAccount healthCheck status first

Scam red flags

Most scams share the same tells.

Watch for

  • Unrealistic pay or a vague description — 'too good to be true'.
  • Pressure to talk off-platform before a contract exists.
  • Requests for free work dressed up as a 'test task'.
  • Any request to pay, or be paid, outside Upwork.
Trust your gut
If it feels off, it is. Keeping all comms and payment on Upwork is the fastest scam filter and keeps protection intact.

Reporting & blocking

Flagging bad actors protects you and everyone else.

What to report

  • Pre-contract contact sharing, off-platform payment requests, fake profiles, copyrighted logos, or threats.
  • Reports are confidential.
  • You can also block and unblock users.

Secure your account

Layer your defenses — the account is the whole business.

Security layers

  • Password (required), security question, and a mobile number.
  • An authenticator app is the highest level Upwork offers — enable it.
  • Manage everything in Account Settings → Password and security.

Avoid phishing

  • Only enter credentials on the real Upwork site or app.
  • Verify an email is legitimate before clicking.
  • Upwork will not ask you to move payment off-platform.

Health, support & status

Know how to check standing and get help.

Account health & help

  • Check account health in settings.
  • You can reactivate a freelancer account, fix login issues, and appeal a suspension.
  • Reach support from the Help Center (Get Support / log in).

When the platform misbehaves

  • Upwork publishes a live system-status page (marketplace, messages, payments).
  • Check it before assuming an app-side bug.
  • Debug order: Upwork Status → account health → the ops-hub.

★ Key takeaways

  • Scam tells: off-platform pressure, free 'tests', unrealistic pay — keep everything on Upwork.
  • Report and block bad actors; reports are confidential.
  • Enable an authenticator app and only log in on the real Upwork.
  • Check account health; you can reactivate, fix login, and appeal suspensions.
  • When something breaks, check Upwork Status first, then account, then the app.
